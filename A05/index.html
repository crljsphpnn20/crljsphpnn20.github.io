Inc Museum

The INC Museum is a modern cultural and religious museum located in Quezon City, Philippines. Operated by the Iglesia Ni Cristo, it showcases the Church's history, doctrines, and global expansion through interactive exhibits, historical artifacts, and multimedia displays. The museum highlights the legacy of INC founder Felix Y. Manalo and serves as an educational destination for both members and visitors.

Punta Sta Ana, Manila

The Iglesia ni Cristo Museum, located in Punta, Santa Ana, Manila, is a historic site operated by the Iglesia ni Cristo. Originally built as a place of worship, it holds deep significance as the location where Felix Manalo, the church’s founder, first preached about the Iglesia ni Cristo in 1914. Now serving as a museum, the building preserves the early history and legacy of the church, showcasing artifacts, documents, and displays related to its founding and growth.

Brother. Felix Manalo landmark

Felix Y. Manalo, the founder of the Iglesia ni Cristo, is buried at the INC Central Temple complex in Tandang Sora, Quezon City, Philippines. His tomb is located within the grounds of the Central Office, where a mausoleum was built in his honor. This site serves not only as his final resting place but also as a significant landmark for members of the Iglesia ni Cristo.

Honolulu Hawaii

The first worship service of the Iglesia ni Cristo (INC) in Honolulu, Hawaii was held on July 27, 1968. This marked the beginning of the Church's mission outside the Philippines, making Hawaii the first overseas locale of the INC. The worship service was conducted by Brother Eraño G. Manalo, then Executive Minister, signifying a major milestone in the Church’s global expansion. Honolulu remains historically significant as the launching point of the Iglesia ni Cristo’s international mission.

Gallery

Discover some of the most treasured artifacts preserved in the INC Museum that represent heritage, faith, and history.

INC Central Model

The INC Central Church model in the museum is a scale replica of the Central Temple, showcasing its iconic design and symbolizing the Church’s unity and growth.

Wooden Mural

The wooden mural at the INC Museum, unveiled in 1984, depicts key events in Iglesia ni Cristo history, crafted from materials of the Central Temple.

Debate at the Plaza

Debates in town plazas allowed INC ministers to defend the Church’s Bible-based beliefs against critics, demonstrating the Church's commitment to truth.

Visit Our Location

The Iglesia Ni Cristo Museum is located within the Central Office Complex in Quezon City, Philippines. Easily accessible via major roads and public transport, the museum offers a peaceful and secure environment where visitors can explore the Church’s rich history and heritage.
