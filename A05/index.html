INC MUSEUM

The INC Museum is a modern cultural and religious museum located in Quezon City, Philippines. Operated by the Iglesia Ni Cristo, it showcases the Church's history, doctrines, and global expansion through interactive exhibits, historical artifacts, and multimedia displays. The museum highlights the legacy of INC founder Felix Y. Manalo and serves as an educational destination for both members and visitors.

History

The INC Museum was established by the Iglesia Ni Cristo (Church Of Christ) to preserve and showcase its rich religious and cultural heritage. The idea began during the leadership of Eraño G. Manalo and was fully realized under Executive Minister Eduardo V. Manalo. The current museum building was inaugurated on August 26, 2015, as part of the Church’s centennial projects. Located within the INC Central Complex in Quezon City, it features modern architecture and interactive exhibits that chronicle the Church’s history—from its founding by Felix Y. Manalo in 1914 to its global expansion.

The Birthsite of Brother Felix Y. Manalo, located in Barangay Tipas, Taguig City, is a historical landmark commemorating the birthplace of the Iglesia Ni Cristo's first Executive Minister. It serves as a tribute to his legacy and the beginnings of the Church's establishment in the Philippines.

The first Iglesia Ni Cristo church in Honolulu, Hawaii, established in 1968, marks the beginning of the Church’s mission outside the Philippines. It symbolizes the Church’s expanding global reach and spiritual mission worldwide.

The INC Museum Extension in Sta. Ana is a preserved site showcasing early Iglesia Ni Cristo activities. Featuring original structures and exhibits, it honors the Church’s foundational years and historical impact.

Visit Our Location

The Iglesia Ni Cristo Museum is located within the Central Office Complex in Quezon City, Philippines. Easily accessible via major roads and public transport, the museum offers a peaceful and secure environment where visitors can explore the Church’s rich history and heritage.
